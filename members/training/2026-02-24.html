Playing catch is the most fundamental skill in baseball, and we do it at every practice and before each game. But there's a big difference between going through the motions and playing catch with intent. I've heard it said a few times that the quickest way to identify a pro baseball player is to watch how they play catch. The first two videos break down what it means to catch with purpose, especially the satisfying (and important!) pop you should be working toward every throw. The third video is a variation on the actual catch routine we run at practice, straight from the guy who built it: Trent Mongero.
